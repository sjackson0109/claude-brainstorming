# Human-centred Design Brainstorming Skill

## Purpose

The Human-centred Design Brainstorming Skill specialises in user-focused design methodology, empathy-driven innovation, and systematic understanding of human needs and behaviours. This skill empowers strategic thinking through human-centered research, participatory design processes, and the creation of solutions that prioritise user experience, accessibility, and meaningful human interaction across products, services, and systems.

## Core Capabilities

### User Research and Empathy Building
- **User Research and Ethnographic Studies**: Conduct comprehensive user research including interviews, observations, and ethnographic studies understanding user needs, behaviours, and contexts
- **Empathy Mapping and Persona Development**: Create detailed user personas and empathy maps capturing user motivations, pain points, and emotional experiences
- **Journey Mapping and Experience Design**: Map user journeys identifying touchpoints, emotions, and opportunities for experience improvement

### Participatory Design and Co-creation
- **Co-design and Participatory Methods**: Implement co-design sessions and participatory workshops engaging users directly in solution development and validation
- **Stakeholder Engagement and Community Involvement**: Create inclusive design processes involving diverse stakeholders and community members in design decision-making
- **Design Thinking Facilitation**: Facilitate design thinking workshops and human-centered innovation sessions generating user-focused solutions

### Experience Design and Usability Optimisation
- **User Experience (UX) Design and Architecture**: Design intuitive user experiences including information architecture, interaction design, and usability optimisation
- **Accessibility and Inclusive Design**: Implement universal design principles ensuring accessibility for users with diverse abilities and backgrounds
- **Prototyping and User Testing**: Create rapid prototypes and conduct user testing validating design concepts and iterating based on user feedback

## Implementation Framework

### Human-centred Design Process

**Phase 1: Human-centred Research and Insight Generation (120 minutes)**
- Conduct user research using multiple methods including interviews, surveys, observations, and contextual inquiry
- Analyse user data identifying patterns, needs, and design opportunities including unmet needs and pain points
- Create user personas and empathy maps representing different user segments and their characteristics
- Map current user experiences identifying touchpoints, emotions, and opportunities for improvement

**Phase 2: Ideation and Co-creation (90 minutes)**
- Facilitate ideation sessions focused on addressing identified user needs and improving user experience
- Engage users and stakeholders in co-design activities generating solutions collaboratively
- Use human-centered design tools including storyboarding, scenario planning, and experience prototyping
- Prioritise design concepts based on user value, feasibility, and alignment with human needs

**Phase 3: Prototyping and Validation (90 minutes)**
- Create low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes enabling user interaction and feedback collection
- Conduct user testing sessions observing user behaviour and gathering feedback on design concepts
- Iterate design solutions based on user feedback and usability testing results
- Validate design solutions against user needs and accessibility requirements
