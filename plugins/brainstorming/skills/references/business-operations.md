# Business Operations Brainstorming

## Activation Triggers

This skill responds to business operations brainstorming requests:

- "Brainstorm process design for [workflow]"
- "Help me brainstorm workflow optimisation for [department]"
- "Let's brainstorm internal systems for [function]"
- "Brainstorm operational planning for [initiative]"
- "Help me brainstorm efficiency improvements for [process]"
- "Let's brainstorm team structure for [organisation]"
- "Brainstorm resource allocation for [project]"
- "Help me brainstorm quality control processes"

## Scope

**What this covers:**
- Business process design and workflow optimisation
- Internal systems and operational frameworks
- Team structure and resource allocation
- Quality control and performance measurement
- Efficiency improvements and automation opportunities
- Project management and execution planning
- Communication and collaboration processes

**What this doesn't cover:**
- Product features and development (use Product Development Brainstorming)
- Technical system architecture (use Technical Architecture Brainstorming)
- Market strategy and customer-facing activities (use Commercial Strategy Brainstorming)
- Long-term strategic direction (use Strategic Planning Brainstorming)

## Conversation Rules

1. **One question at a time** - Focus on specific operational decisions
2. **Multiple choice preferred** - Present process options for easier evaluation
3. **Lead with recommendation** - Propose 2-3 approaches, recommend one with reasoning
4. **Efficiency focused** - Optimise for simplicity and effectiveness
5. **Validate incrementally** - Present operational sections, check practical alignment

## Process

**Understanding the operational challenge:**
- Current state of processes and pain points
- Team capabilities and resource constraints
- Performance goals and success metrics
- Integration requirements with existing systems

**Exploring operational approaches:**
- Propose 2-3 different process designs with trade-offs
- Consider implementation complexity and change management
- Evaluate resource requirements and timeline

**Presenting the solution:**
- Break into logical process steps and responsibilities
- Focus on practical, implementable improvements
- Validate operational feasibility after each section

## Key Frameworks

**Operations Planning Questions:**
- "What's the current process and where are the main bottlenecks?"
- "Who's involved and what are their current roles and responsibilities?"
- "What are your performance goals and how will you measure success?"
- "What constraints do you have in terms of resources, budget, and timeline?"

**Process Design:**
- Workflow mapping and bottleneck identification
- Role definition and responsibility assignment
- Performance measurement and quality control
- Communication and collaboration protocols

**Output Focus:**
- Clear process flows with defined steps and owners
- Practical efficiency improvements with measurable impact
- Implementable operational framework
- Change management plan and success metrics
