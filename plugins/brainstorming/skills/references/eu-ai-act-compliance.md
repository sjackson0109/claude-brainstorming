# EU AI Act Compliance Brainstorming Skill

## Purpose

The EU AI Act Compliance Brainstorming Skill specialises in artificial intelligence regulatory compliance, risk management, and governance frameworks required under the European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act. This skill empowers strategic thinking through systematic AI system classification, compliance strategy development, and the creation of comprehensive governance frameworks ensuring legal compliance while enabling responsible AI innovation and deployment.

## Core Capabilities

### AI System Classification and Risk Assessment
- **AI System Risk Categorisation**: Systematically classify AI systems according to EU AI Act risk categories including prohibited, high-risk, limited risk, and minimal risk classifications
- **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment**: Conduct comprehensive assessments of AI system impacts on fundamental rights including privacy, non-discrimination, and human dignity
- **Algorithmic Transparency and Explainability**: Implement algorithmic transparency requirements including documentation, explainability, and auditability standards

### Compliance Framework Development and Implementation
- **Legal Compliance Strategy**: Develop comprehensive compliance strategies addressing all relevant EU AI Act requirements including technical documentation, conformity assessment, and ongoing monitoring
- **Quality Management System Implementation**: Create quality management systems for AI development and deployment meeting EU AI Act standards and requirements
- **Third-Party Conformity Assessment**: Navigate conformity assessment procedures including notified body involvement and CE marking requirements

### AI Governance and Risk Management
- **AI Governance Structure Design**: Establish AI governance structures including roles, responsibilities, and accountability frameworks for AI compliance and risk management
- **Continuous Monitoring and Audit**: Implement continuous monitoring systems ensuring ongoing compliance with EU AI Act requirements throughout AI system lifecycle
- **Incident Management and Reporting**: Create incident management procedures including serious incident reporting and regulatory notification requirements

## Implementation Framework

### EU AI Act Compliance Assessment Process

**Phase 1: AI System Inventory and Classification (90 minutes)**
- Conduct comprehensive inventory of all AI systems including development, deployment, and third-party AI technologies
- Classify AI systems according to EU AI Act risk categories and determine applicable compliance requirements
- Assess AI system impacts on fundamental rights and identify potential discrimination, bias, or privacy risks
- Map AI system data flows, decision-making processes, and human oversight mechanisms

**Phase 2: Compliance Gap Analysis and Strategy Development (120 minutes)**
- Conduct detailed gap analysis comparing current AI practices with EU AI Act compliance requirements
- Develop comprehensive compliance roadmap including timeline, resources, and implementation priorities
- Design quality management systems and technical documentation meeting EU AI Act standards
- Create AI governance framework including roles, responsibilities, and accountability mechanisms

**Phase 3: Implementation and Continuous Compliance (90 minutes)**
- Implement technical compliance measures including algorithmic impact assessments, bias testing, and transparency documentation
- Establish ongoing monitoring and audit procedures ensuring continuous compliance throughout AI system lifecycle
- Create training programmes and awareness initiatives ensuring organisational understanding of AI compliance requirements
- Design incident management and regulatory reporting procedures for serious incidents and compliance breaches

### EU AI Act Compliance Categories and Requirements

**High-Risk AI System Compliance**
- **Safety and Fundamental Rights Protection**: Implement robust safety measures and fundamental rights protection safeguards
- **Human Oversight and Intervention**: Design appropriate human oversight mechanisms ensuring meaningful human control over AI decisions
- **Technical Documentation and Conformity Assessment**: Create comprehensive technical documentation and undergo conformity assessment procedures

**AI Transparency and Limited Risk System Compliance**
- **Transparency and Disclosure Requirements**: Implement transparency measures ensuring users understand AI system capabilities and limitations
- **Manipulation and Deception Prevention**: Design safeguards preventing AI system use for manipulation or deception
- **Biometric and Emotion Recognition Compliance**: Implement specific requirements for biometric identification and emotion recognition systems

**Prohibited AI Practice Avoidance**
- **Social Scoring and Manipulation Prevention**: Ensure AI systems do not engage in prohibited social scoring or subliminal manipulation
- **Vulnerable Group Protection**: Implement safeguards protecting children and vulnerable populations from potential AI harms
- **Real-Time Biometric Restrictions**: Understand and comply with restrictions on real-time remote biometric identification
