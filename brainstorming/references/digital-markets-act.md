# Digital Markets Act (DMA) Compliance Brainstorming Skill

## Purpose

The Digital Markets Act (DMA) Compliance Brainstorming Skill specialises in European digital market regulation compliance, platform fairness implementation, and digital ecosystem strategy development. This skill empowers strategic thinking through systematic DMA requirement analysis, gatekeeper obligation management, and the creation of fair, competitive digital market strategies that comply with European Union digital regulation while maintaining business effectiveness.

## Core Capabilities

### Gatekeeper Assessment and Compliance Framework
- **Gatekeeper Status Evaluation**: Systematically assess whether digital platforms meet DMA gatekeeper designation criteria and obligations
- **Core Platform Service Analysis**: Analyse core platform services including search engines, social networks, and digital advertising to ensure DMA compliance
- **Market Position and Impact Assessment**: Evaluate market position, user reach, and economic impact to understand DMA applicability and requirements

### Platform Fairness and Interoperability Implementation
- **Interoperability Requirements**: Implement technical and business interoperability enabling fair access and competition within digital platforms
- **Self-Preferencing Prevention**: Design systems preventing unfair self-preferencing while maintaining platform effectiveness and user experience
- **Business User Fairness**: Create fair treatment frameworks for business users including transparent terms and non-discriminatory access

### Data Portability and User Control Framework
- **Data Portability Implementation**: Create comprehensive data portability systems enabling users to transfer data between platforms efficiently
- **User Choice and Control**: Implement user choice mechanisms including consent management and service selection alternatives
- **Transparency and Reporting**: Establish comprehensive transparency frameworks including algorithmic transparency and compliance reporting

## Implementation Framework

### DMA Compliance Development Process

**Phase 1: DMA Applicability Assessment and Gap Analysis (90 minutes)**
- Assess potential gatekeeper status based on quantitative thresholds including users, turnover, and market capitalisation
- Map core platform services and evaluate DMA obligations applicable to specific platform functionalities
- Conduct comprehensive gap analysis comparing current practices with DMA requirements
- Assess compliance timeline and resource requirements for DMA obligation implementation

**Phase 2: Compliance Strategy Design and Implementation Planning (120 minutes)**
- Design comprehensive DMA compliance strategy addressing all applicable obligations and requirements
- Create technical implementation plans for interoperability, data portability, and platform fairness requirements
- Develop business process changes ensuring ongoing DMA compliance in platform operations
- Design monitoring and reporting systems for regulatory compliance demonstration

**Phase 3: Implementation and Ongoing Compliance Management (90 minutes)**
- Implement DMA compliance measures including technical changes and business process modifications
- Establish ongoing monitoring systems ensuring continued DMA compliance as platform evolves
- Create stakeholder communication strategies including business user engagement and regulatory coordination
- Implement continuous improvement processes based on regulatory feedback and market evolution

### DMA Obligation Implementation Areas

**Platform Fairness and Non-Discrimination (Articles 5-6)**
- **Fair Treatment of Business Users**: Ensure non-discriminatory treatment of business users and fair access to platform capabilities
- **Self-Preferencing Prevention**: Implement systems preventing unfair preference of gatekeeper's own services over competitors
- **Transparency in Platform Operations**: Provide clear information about platform operations, algorithms, and decision-making processes

**Interoperability and Data Portability (Article 6)**
- **Messaging Service Interoperability**: Implement interoperability for messaging services enabling cross-platform communication
- **Social Network Data Portability**: Create data portability systems for social networking data enabling user migration between platforms
- **Technical Interface Provision**: Provide technical interfaces enabling fair and reasonable access to platform capabilities

**User Choice and Control (Articles 5-6)**
- **Default Service Selection**: Allow users to choose default services including browsers, search engines, and virtual assistants
- **Consent and Privacy Controls**: Implement granular consent mechanisms and privacy controls giving users meaningful choice
- **Service Bundling Restrictions**: Avoid tying or bundling services in ways that restrict user choice and market competition
