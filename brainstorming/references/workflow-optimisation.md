# Workflow optimisation

## Purpose
Workflow optimisation provides systematic approaches to analysing, improving, and streamlining business processes to maximise efficiency, reduce waste, improve quality, and enhance overall organisational performance. This brainstorming skill enables teams to identify bottlenecks, eliminate redundancies, automate routine tasks, and design workflows that support strategic objectives while improving employee productivity and customer satisfaction.

## Core Capabilities
- **Process Analysis and Mapping**: Comprehensive analysis and visualization of current workflows to understand process flows, dependencies, and performance characteristics
- **Bottleneck Identification and Resolution**: Systematic identification of process bottlenecks and constraint points with targeted solutions to improve throughput and efficiency
- **Waste Elimination and Lean optimisation**: Application of lean principles to eliminate various types of waste including time, resources, and effort while maintaining or improving output quality
- **Automation Integration and Design**: Strategic integration of automation technologies to streamline routine tasks and improve process consistency and speed
- **Performance Measurement and Analytics**: Implementation of metrics and analytics systems that monitor workflow performance and identify continuous improvement opportunities
- **Change Management and Adoption**: Effective management of workflow changes including stakeholder engagement, training, and adoption strategies
- **Cross-Functional Coordination**: optimisation of workflows that span multiple departments or functions to improve integration and eliminate handoff inefficiencies
- **Quality Assurance and Error Reduction**: Design of workflows that inherently reduce errors, improve quality control, and ensure consistent output standards

- **Workflow Mapping and Documentation": Comprehensive mapping of current workflows including tasks, decision points, handoffs, and timing
