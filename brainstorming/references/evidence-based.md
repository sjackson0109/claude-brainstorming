# Evidence-based Brainstorming Skill

## Purpose

The Evidence-based Brainstorming Skill specialises in research-driven idea generation, fact-supported creative thinking, and systematic validation of brainstorming outcomes. This skill empowers strategic thinking through systematic evidence collection, research-informed ideation, and the creation of robust validation frameworks ensuring brainstorming results are grounded in credible evidence, empirical data, and proven best practices.

## Core Capabilities

### Research-Driven Idea Foundation
- **Systematic Literature and Research Review**: Conduct comprehensive research including academic studies, industry reports, and empirical evidence supporting brainstorming objectives
- **Evidence Collection and Synthesis**: Gather, analyse, and synthesise diverse evidence sources including quantitative data, qualitative insights, and expert knowledge
- **Research Quality Assessment**: Evaluate research quality including methodology, sample size, credibility, and applicability to brainstorming context

### Data-Informed Creative Thinking
- **Evidence-Based Ideation**: Generate ideas systematically grounded in research findings, empirical data, and proven principles rather than unsupported assumptions
- **Hypothesis-Driven Brainstorming**: Structure brainstorming around testable hypotheses that can be validated through research and empirical evidence
- **Research Gap Identification**: Identify knowledge gaps and research opportunities informing innovative thinking and novel solution development

### Validation and Verification Framework
- **Idea Validation Methodology**: Implement systematic validation processes testing brainstorming outcomes against available evidence and empirical data
- **Fact-Checking and Verification**: Create rigorous fact-checking processes ensuring accuracy and credibility of brainstorming outputs
- **Evidence Quality Grading**: Establish evidence quality assessment criteria evaluating strength and reliability of supporting research

## Implementation Framework

### Evidence-based Brainstorming Process

**Phase 1: Research Foundation and Evidence Collection (120 minutes)**
- Define research questions and information needs supporting brainstorming objectives and desired outcomes
- Conduct systematic literature review including academic research, industry studies, and expert publications
- Gather empirical data from reliable sources including government statistics, industry databases, and credible research organisations
- Assess research quality and credibility using established criteria including methodology, peer review, and replication

**Phase 2: Evidence Analysis and Insight Generation (90 minutes)**
- Analyse collected evidence identifying patterns, trends, and insights relevant to brainstorming objectives
- Synthesise research findings creating evidence-based foundation for creative thinking and idea generation
- Identify research gaps and conflicting evidence informing areas requiring further investigation or innovative approaches
- Create evidence summary and reference documentation supporting brainstorming process and outputs

**Phase 3: Evidence-Informed Ideation and Validation (90 minutes)**
- Generate ideas systematically grounded in research findings and empirical evidence rather than speculation
- Test generated ideas against available evidence identifying supporting research and potential contradictions
- Develop implementation frameworks incorporating evidence-based best practices and proven methodologies
- Create validation criteria and testing procedures ensuring ongoing evidence-based refinement of ideas

### Evidence Sources and Research Categories

**Academic and Scholarly Research**
- **Peer-Reviewed Publications**: Access peer-reviewed academic journals and conference proceedings providing rigorous research findings
- **Meta-Analyses and Systematic Reviews**: Utilise meta-analyses and systematic reviews providing consolidated evidence across multiple studies
- **University and Research Institution Studies**: Leverage research from credible universities and research institutions with appropriate expertise

**Industry and Market Intelligence**
- **Industry Research and Reports**: Use authoritative industry research from recognised consulting firms and trade associations
- **Market Data and Analytics**: Access market research, customer surveys, and industry analytics providing empirical market insights
- **Case Studies and Best Practice Documentation**: Review documented case studies and best practices from similar organisations and industries

**Government and Official Statistics**
- **Official Statistics and Census Data**: Utilise government statistics, census data, and official demographic information
- **Regulatory and Policy Research**: Access regulatory studies, policy evaluations, and government research supporting evidence-based decision making
- **International Organisation Research**: Leverage research from international organisations including World Bank, OECD, and United Nations agencies

## Performance Measurement

### Research Quality and Comprehensiveness
- **Evidence Coverage and Depth**: Assess comprehensiveness of research coverage including breadth of sources and depth of analysis
- **Research Quality Assessment**: Evaluate quality of research sources including peer review status, methodology rigor, and sample size adequacy
- **Citation and Reference Accuracy**: Monitor accuracy and completeness of citations and references supporting brainstorming outcomes

### Validation Effectiveness and Accuracy
- **Idea Validation Success Rate**: Track success rate of evidence-based validation identifying ideas likely to succeed based on supporting research
- **Fact-Checking Accuracy**: Measure accuracy of fact-checking processes and identification of misinformation or unsupported claims
- **Evidence-Outcome Correlation**: Assess correlation between evidence quality and brainstorming outcome success and implementation effectiveness

### Implementation Impact and Learning
- **Evidence-Based Implementation Success**: Track success of evidence-based implementations compared to intuition-based or assumption-driven approaches
- **Research Learning and Knowledge Development**: Monitor organisational learning and knowledge development resulting from evidence-based brainstorming practices
- **Expert Validation and Peer Review**: Evaluate expert validation and peer review feedback enhancing evidence-based thinking quality and credibility

- **Research Synthesis Skills**: Develop strong research synthesis capabilities integrating diverse evidence sources into coherent insights and recommendations
- **Evidence-Practice Gap Bridging**: Bridge evidence-practice gaps translating research findings into practical and implementable solutions

The Evidence-based Brainstorming Skill represents essential capability for creating credible, reliable, and implementable brainstorming outcomes. Through systematic application of research methodology and evidence validation, this skill enhances brainstorming quality while building confidence and trust in creative thinking processes and their resulting solutions.
