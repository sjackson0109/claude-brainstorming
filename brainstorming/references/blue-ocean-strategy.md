# Blue Ocean Strategy - Brainstorming Skill

## Purpose
Develop systematic approaches to create uncontested market space and make competition irrelevant by simultaneously pursuing differentiation and low cost, moving beyond traditional competitive boundaries.

## Core Capabilities

### Blue Ocean Framework
- **Value Innovation**: Create new value for buyers while reducing costs
- **Strategy Canvas**: Visual representation of competitive factors and value propositions
- **Four Actions Framework**: Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create framework for strategy formulation
- **Six Paths to Blue Oceans**: Systematic exploration of new market opportunities

### Red Ocean vs. Blue Ocean Analysis

#### Current Market Assessment
```
Red Ocean Characteristics:
- Intense competition in existing market space
- Beat the competition mindset
- Exploit existing demand
- Value-cost trade-off
- Align activities with strategic choice

Blue Ocean Opportunities:
- Create uncontested market space
- Make competition irrelevant
- Create and capture new demand
- Break value-cost trade-off
- Align system of activities in pursuit of differentiation and low cost
```

#### Market Space Exploration
- **Industry Boundaries**: Look across alternative industries
- **Strategic Groups**: Examine different strategic groups within industry
- **Buyer Groups**: Focus on different buyer groups
- **Complementary Offerings**: Explore complementary product and service offerings
- **Functional vs. Emotional Appeal**: Balance functional and emotional value propositions
- **Time Trends**: Participate in shaping external trends over time

### Value Innovation Process

#### Buyer Value Elements
- **Price**: Cost considerations and value for money
- **Convenience**: Ease of purchase, use, and maintenance
- **Risk**: Reduction of financial, physical, and credibility risks
- **Brand**: Image and reputation considerations
- **Quality**: Product/service performance and reliability
- **Customization**: Personalization and specific needs fulfillment

#### Cost Structure Innovation
- **Operational Efficiency**: Streamline operations to reduce costs
- **Resource optimisation**: More efficient use of existing resources
- **Process Simplification**: Eliminate unnecessary complexity
- **Technology Leverage**: Use technology to reduce operational costs
- **Supply Chain Innovation**: New approaches to sourcing and distribution
- **Organizational Design**: Structural changes to reduce overhead

### Systematic Blue Ocean Discovery

#### Six Paths to Blue Ocean

**Path 1: Alternative Industries**
- Look across substitute industries that compete for same buyer group
- Identify alternatives that buyers consider when making purchase decisions
- Study why buyers trade across alternatives
- Develop bridges between alternative industries

**Path 2: Strategic Groups**
- Examine different strategic groups within same industry
- Understand factors that influence buyer decisions between groups
- Identify opportunity to capture essence of both groups
- Create new strategic position that bridges groups

**Path 3: Chain of Buyers**
- Challenge conventional definition of target buyer
- Explore influencers, purchasers, and users in buying chain
- Shift focus to previously overlooked buyer group
- Create value for entire buying chain

**Path 4: Complementary Products/Services**
- Look beyond industry's product and service boundaries
- Identify complementary products and services used with offering
- Understand total solution buyers seek
- Integrate complementary elements into core offering

**Path 5: Functional vs. Emotional Orientation**
- Challenge industry's functional or emotional orientation
- Add emotional appeal to functionally oriented industry
- Strip away emotional elements to create functional focus
- Find new balance between functional and emotional appeals

**Path 6: Time and Trends**
- Look across time at trends affecting industry
- Identify how trends will change value delivery
- Understand how to shape trends to create new market space
- Develop offerings that address future rather than current needs

### Strategy Formulation Tools

#### Strategy Canvas Development
```
Strategy Canvas Components:
- Competing Factors: Key variables on which industry competes
- Offering Level: Degree to which offering delivers each factor
- Value Curve: Line connecting offering levels across factors
- Competitive Convergence: Areas where all players offer similar value
- Differentiation Opportunities: Factors where new value can be created
```

#### Four Actions Framework Application
- **Eliminate**: Which factors should be eliminated that industry takes for granted?
- **Reduce**: Which factors should be reduced below industry standard?
- **Raise**: Which factors should be raised above industry standard?
- **Create**: Which factors should be created that industry has never offered?

### Risk Management

#### Blue Ocean Risks
- **Market Acceptance**: Risk that new value proposition won't resonate with customers
- **Execution Capability**: Risk of inability to deliver on blue ocean promise
- **Competitive Response**: Risk of rapid competitive copying
- **Resource Requirements**: Risk of underestimating investment needed
- **Timing**: Risk of being too early or too late to market

#### Mitigation Strategies
- **Market Testing**: Validate blue ocean opportunities before full commitment
- **Phased Rollout**: Gradual market entry to test and refine approach
- **Capability Building**: Develop necessary skills and resources
- **Patent Protection**: Protect intellectual property where possible
- **Speed to Market**: Move quickly to establish market position
