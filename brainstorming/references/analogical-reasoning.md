# Analogical Reasoning System - Brainstorming Skill

## Purpose
Develop systematic approaches to leverage analogical thinking for problem-solving, strategy development, and innovation by identifying structural similarities across different domains and contexts.

## Core Capabilities

### Analogical Thinking Framework
- **Pattern Recognition**: Identify abstract structural patterns that transfer across domains
- **Relational Mapping**: Map relationships and connections between source and target domains
- **Similarity Assessment**: Evaluate the quality and relevance of analogical connections
- **Transfer Mechanisms**: Apply insights from familiar domains to novel problems

### Types of Analogical Reasoning

#### Surface vs. Structural Analogies
- **Surface Analogies**: Superficial similarities in appearance or attributes
- **Structural Analogies**: Deep similarities in relationships and causal structures
- **Systematic Analogies**: Complex systems with multiple aligned relations
- **Pragmatic Analogies**: Purpose-driven similarities for specific problem-solving goals

#### Cross-Domain Knowledge Transfer
- **Inter-Industry Learning**: Apply successful strategies from one industry to another
- **Cross-Functional Insights**: Transfer approaches between different business functions
- **Historical Analogies**: Learn from past situations and events
- **Biological Analogies**: Apply natural systems and processes to human problems

### Analogical Discovery Process

#### Source Domain Identification
```
Selection Criteria:
1. Problem Structure Similarity
2. Solution Mechanism Relevance
3. Causal Relationship Alignment
4. Constraint Pattern Matching
5. Success Factor Transferability
```

#### Mapping Mechanisms
- **One-to-One Mapping**: Direct correspondence between elements
- **Many-to-One Mapping**: Multiple source elements map to single target
- **Systematic Mapping**: Coherent system of correspondences
- **Partial Mapping**: Incomplete but useful correspondences

### Common Pitfalls and Mitigation

#### Superficial Analogies
- **Surface Feature Focus**: Avoid being misled by superficial similarities
- **Structural Analysis**: Always examine deeper relational patterns
- **Multiple Analogies**: Use several analogies to triangulate insights
- **Critical Questioning**: Systematically challenge analogical assumptions

#### Over-Extension
- **Scope Limitations**: recognise where analogies break down
- **Context Differences**: Account for important contextual variations
- **Partial Application**: Use parts of analogies rather than wholesale transfer
- **Iterative Adaptation**: Modify analogical solutions to fit target context
