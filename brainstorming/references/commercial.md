# Commercial Strategy Brainstorming

## Activation

This skill responds to commercial and revenue-focused brainstorming requests:

**Primary triggers:**
- "Brainstorm marketing strategy for [product/campaign]"
- "Help me brainstorm a sales process for [offering]"
- "Let's brainstorm a go-to-market strategy for [launch]"
- "Brainstorm customer acquisition for [business]"
- "Help me brainstorm pricing strategy for [product]"

**Campaign & positioning:**
- "Let's brainstorm a marketing campaign for [audience]"
- "Help me brainstorm brand positioning for [market]"
- "Brainstorm content strategy for [channel]"

**Revenue & growth:**
- "Brainstorm revenue models for [business]"
- "Help me brainstorm expansion strategy for [market]"
- "Let's brainstorm partnership strategy for [growth]"

## Overview

Transform business ideas into executable commercial strategies through structured dialogue. Focus on:

- **Go-to-market strategies** - Target markets, positioning, launch plans
- **Sales processes** - Lead generation, qualification, closing
- **Marketing campaigns** - Messaging, channels, content, measurement
- **Customer acquisition** - Funnels, conversion, retention
- **Revenue strategies** - Pricing, packaging, monetisation
- **Brand positioning** - Differentiation, value props, competitive advantage

Start by understanding your commercial context, then ask focused questions to refine the strategy. Present options with clear trade-offs and recommendations.

## The Process

**Understanding the opportunity:**
- Current market position and competitive landscape
- Target customers and their needs/pain points  
- Value proposition and differentiation
- Revenue goals and success metrics

**Exploring approaches:**
- Propose 2-3 different strategies with pros/cons
- Lead with recommended option and reasoning
- Consider resource constraints and market timing

**Presenting the strategy:**
- Break into digestible sections (200-300 words each)
- Validate alignment after each section
- Adapt to specific deliverable type:
  - **Go-to-market**: Segments, positioning, channels, timeline
  - **Sales strategy**: Process, qualification, objection handling
  - **Marketing campaigns**: Audience, messaging, channels, budget
  - **Revenue strategy**: Models, pricing, growth levers

## Key Principles

- **Customer-first thinking** - Start with customer needs, not internal capabilities
- **Evidence-based decisions** - Support strategies with market data and research
- **Incremental validation** - Test assumptions before full commitment
- **Clear differentiation** - Articulate unique value and competitive advantage
- **Measurable outcomes** - Define success metrics and tracking methods
- **Resource realistic** - Match strategy ambition to available resources
